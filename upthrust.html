Frequently Asked Questions on Upthrust in Fluids, Archimedes’ Principle and Floatation

What is meant by the term buoyancy?

Buoyancy is the property of liquid to exert an upward force on a body immersed in it.

Define upthrust?

Upthrust is also known as the buoyant force. It is the upward force acting on a body when it is partially or fully immersed in a liquid.

Why is a force needed to keep a block of wood inside water?

An upward force, an upthrust is required to keep a block of wood in water as the upthrust due to water on the block when it is completely submerged is much more than its weight.

State Archimedes’ principle?

Archimedes’ principle states that when a body is immersed partially or completely in a liquid, it experiences an upthrust which is equal to the weight of the liquid displaced by it.

Define the term density?

The density of a substance is its mass per unit volume, i.e., the density of a substance

What is the unit of relative density?

Relative density is no unit.
